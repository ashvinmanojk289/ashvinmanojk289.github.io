  • Featured Research Project

    Multimodal Graph Learning for Early Alzheimer’s Disease Prediction

    Graph learning / medical AI / multimodal fusion

    Aug 2025 - May 2026

    Research-oriented framework for early Alzheimer’s Disease prediction using structural MRI, DTI tractography, and SNP genetic data. The architecture combines graph neural networks, graph transformers, attention-guided modality fusion, and salient node selection.

    • Graph Neural Networks
    • Graph Transformers
    • Attention fusion
    • DTI tractography

    Problem

    Early diagnosis of Alzheimer’s Disease requires models that can reason across structural, diffusion, and genetic signals rather than relying on a single modality.

    Solution

    This ongoing project designs a multimodal deep learning pipeline that fuses MRI, DTI, and SNP features with graph-based reasoning to classify cognitively normal, mild cognitive impairment, and AD cases more effectively.

    Technical Direction

    The current architecture emphasizes graph neural networks, graph transformers, salient node selection, and attention-guided fusion to preserve disease-relevant structure across modalities.
